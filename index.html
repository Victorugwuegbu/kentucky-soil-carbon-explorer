Kentucky Soil Carbon Explorer

About the Map

This map presents county level soil organic carbon across Kentucky at five depth intervals. It is designed to help users explore how soil carbon varies across the state and with depth, while also relating those patterns to Kentucky’s broader physiographic setting.

How to Use

Use the depth buttons in the header to switch between soil depths. The county colors and legend update automatically. Click a county to view its full soil carbon profile in the info panel. Use the search box to quickly zoom to a county, and turn on the physiographic regions overlay to compare soil carbon patterns with Kentucky’s major landscape regions.

Data and Methods

County level soil values were derived from USDA NRCS Soil Data Access and prepared outside the browser. I queried the public SSURGO database, joined the resulting soil table to Kentucky county boundaries in QGIS, cleaned the fields, and exported the final county layer as GeoJSON for web display. The physiographic regions layer was also prepared in QGIS and added as a contextual overlay.

Classification

The choropleth uses a sequential brown color palette to reflect the soil theme of the map. Class breaks are calculated using Jenks natural breaks, which helps group counties according to the natural distribution of soil carbon values rather than forcing equal interval classes. This supports clearer visual comparison across depth intervals.
